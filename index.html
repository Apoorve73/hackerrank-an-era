Chapter 01 · The Beginning

five years.
not in terms of years,
but in terms of learnings.

The honest version of this story is that I was found.

Third year of college. Writing code at odd hours, pushing things to GitHub the way most people do at that age, not really thinking much of it. Then one day an email shows up from HackerRank. Some script somewhere had apparently picked up my GitHub activity or maybe it was my charisma, who knows ;) and just like that there was this unexpected opportunity sitting in my inbox.

I knew it was a six month internship. I still had a one and a half year of college left after that. I had no idea how it would all work out. I just said yes.

What happened after that I could not have predicted. From the very first Zoom calls I was around people full of energy, full of excitement, genuinely happy to be there. Getting to know everyone's stories, their experience, the way they saw things. It changed something in me quietly. And without really planning it I made some of the best friends of my life. The kind that stick around.

Three of us ended up getting a second internship extension. HackerRank created a new position that didn't even exist before, just for the three of us. It felt genuinely generous in a way I didn't take for granted for a single day. Both intern eras were on CodeScreen and by that second stretch people had started calling me the "senior intern." Honestly that title meant the world to me. It wasn't sarcasm. It was warmth.

By the time I joined full time in January 2023 on the SkillUp team I was stepping into something I wasn't quite sure I was ready for yet. A zero to one product. Building from scratch with no blueprint and no playbook. I don't think many people get that kind of experience early on and I knew how lucky I was. Thinking about architecture, understanding what marketing actually means, growing into a senior role, mentoring people and slowly learning what it really means to make an impact in someone's life. I tried to do that as best I could.

I say I'm spoiled now because I genuinely don't know what I'll find outside. This place became home in a way I didn't see coming. You can reach out to just about anyone and people actually show up for each other. That's rarer than it sounds.

The Moments That Stayed

🔥
Hackathons
Zero clue and all the energy drinks bottled up in a conference room. We always started with nothing and came out with something real. That energy, random people, an idea, a deadline. I'll be chasing it for a while.
✈️
Offsites
Area 83. Mulberry Shades. Discovery Village in 2023. We're a remote team so you'd think it would be nice but performative. It wasn't. I walked in thinking "team bonding thing." I walked out actually bonded. There's a difference.
🤝
Customer Moments
Demoing SkillUp and watching someone get value out of it live in front of you. Being on a call during a product launch window and helping a customer through an issue in real time and getting a genuine thank you from the other end. At some point the team gave me an Extra Mile Award and what I remember most about it is not the award itself but exactly that feeling of a real person's problem actually getting solved. That's the whole point of building things.
🎉
The Celebrations
Cakes for everything. Q3 ARR records. Hackerversaries. The Q3 celebration video in 2021. We were actually happy together, even then. And winning bets with Hari and getting chocolate cake delivered was peak intern life.

Secret Santas

Trek bag, things I had actually asked for. The kind of place where you could be fully yourself. Singing backwards in a work channel and everyone cheering.

The Intern Website

Random Conversations

Chapter 02 · The People

There are people here
I'll be playing board games
with my entire life.

The biggest thing I'm taking from this place isn't anything I shipped. It's the people. And I want to say it to your face, even if your face is a small rectangle on my screen : )

I could try to list everyone but I'd definitely miss someone and feel terrible about it. So instead, a few things that are true:

There's someone who brought me into SkillUp. I don't think they know how much that single moment changed the trajectory of my next three years.
There's someone whose feedback made me sharper every single time. Every time they trusted me with something, I tried to be worthy of it. I hope I was.
There are late-night conversations about product and vision that genuinely changed how I think. I carry those with me.
There's a first reviewer. A first co-author. A last manager. That's not a coincidence. That's what five years of genuinely good collaboration looks like.
There are people who were in the trenches with me every single day. You know who you are. The work we built together. I'll think about all of it.
There's someone who left before me and mentioned my name in their list of people who mattered. Full circle and am just so grateful for that

I have a list I've built over time. 65+ people, including folks who've already moved on from here. If we ever talked, if we ever worked on something together, if you ever made me think differently, you're on it.

Chapter 03 · So Long

I'm not
disappearing.

My last few weeks here were spent writing four knowledge transfer documents: CodeRunner, AuthZ, Next Gen Certifications, infrastructure. Not because anyone asked. Because I've seen what happens when knowledge just disappears with a person, and I didn't want to do that to this team.

I'm leaving with a full heart. There's no word for what I'm going to miss. The random conversations that started at noon and went somewhere unexpected, the voice notes, the memes, the moments that happened without anyone planning them. I'll miss all of it. Genuinely and happily.

This place gave me more than I could have asked for. Good work. Good people. A version of myself I'm proud of. That stays.

Find me wherever you find me. My DMs are open. I'll pick up the phone. lmk if you ever want to catch up, seriously, anytime : )

I'm based in Delhi NCR. So if you're ever passing through, need a place to crash, or just want to grab a coffee with an ex-HackerRanker who will absolutely make you sit through his whole story again. one call away.
